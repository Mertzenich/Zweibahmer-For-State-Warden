John is an insured member of Council Ossian 2073, and he wants to serve as the next State Warden for the Iowa Knights of Columbus.

John for State Warden

IA KofC Elected Office Profile Speech for 2020/2021 Fraternal Year

John's reason he wants to be State Warden is because he knows the importance of the Knights of Columbus in the life of our families, parishes, and communities. He is a family man, married for 30 years to his wife Teresa. They have four children and four grandchildren so they know the importance of faith in the family. He has served as Extraordinary Minister of the Eucharist at his Parish, St. Francis DeSales, and in the Ossian Senior Hospice. He also believes that Iowa is always a contender for the Circle of Honor. He knows how to get it done. In fact, he has done it! John is one of seven Membership Directors that has achieved it and he plans to continue pushing the success in the growth and outreach of our jurisdiction as a State Officer.

About John

John been a Knight since 2008 and just a year later, he became Grand Knight of his Council, a position that he held for the next 7 years until 2016. In 2012-2013 he led the Council to achieve its first Star Council Award in about 9 years. The Council achieved this through implementing new activities in the Council such as the Spelling Bee Contest, a Comedy Night, establishing an electronic phone tree for the council and a Council Newsletter.

From 2013 to 2016 he served as District Warden and from 2015 to 2017 as District Deputy. After attending the Supreme Convention in Philadelphia in 2015 he brought ideas to his District and region to improve the fraternal activities and combining efforts to grow the membership and the outreach of the Knights in northeast Iowa. The Iowa delegation to the Supreme Convention encouraged John and supported him to run as a 3rd Degree Member of the Executive committee in which he served from 2017 to 2019. He was then asked to become the Membership Director for the Iowa Jurisdiction, a position that he held for the same period and in which he led the membership team in Iowa to the Supreme Knight’s Circle of Honor. Iowa had not been on the Circle of Honor since 2013-2014. Iowa was the only Division 2 jurisdiction to achieve such an honor and the second largest jurisdiction in the Order to receive such an honor. Iowa closed the year with 104% of its goal, the largest in almost 10 years. John believes in the growth of Iowa’s membership and he knows how to work with Regional Chairmen, District Deputies and Grand Knights to motivate, encourage and promote the growth of the Order.

John has also brought success to his local Assembly, where he currently serves as Faithful Navigator and has led the Davis Assembly 271 to earn its first ever Civic Award. He also serves as Assistant to the Master of the Fourth Degree in Iowa and is an active Honor guard participant.
